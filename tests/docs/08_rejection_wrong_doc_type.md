# Q3 2026 Infrastructure Cost Analysis Report
**Prepared by:** Finance Department  
**For:** Executive Leadership Team

---

## Executive Summary

Total infrastructure spend for Q3 2026 came in at $4.2 million, which is 8% under budget. Server procurement accounted for $1.1 million. Network upgrades accounted for $800,000. Facilities and power costs were $620,000. Software licensing totalled $980,000. Miscellaneous operational expenses were $700,000.

---

## Cost Breakdown by Category

### Server Procurement
- Dell PowerEdge R750 units x 12: $480,000
- HPE ProLiant DL380 units x 8: $320,000
- Storage arrays (NetApp AFF A400): $300,000

### Network Infrastructure
- Cisco Catalyst 9500 switches x 4: $240,000
- SD-WAN upgrade across 6 offices: $360,000
- Firewall replacement (Palo Alto PA-5450): $200,000

### Software Licensing
- Microsoft 365 Enterprise E5 (2,400 seats): $580,000
- Adobe Creative Cloud (120 seats): $96,000
- Salesforce CRM Enterprise: $304,000

---

## Variance Analysis

The Q3 budget variance of minus $360,000 was driven by delayed server procurement due to supply chain issues. Two Dell units were delivered in Q4 instead of Q3. The network upgrade was also completed 3 weeks ahead of schedule, reducing contractor fees by $45,000.

---

## Q4 Forecast

Q4 forecast is $4.6 million driven by year-end software renewals and planned data centre expansion. The new data centre wing is expected to add $900,000 in one-time capital expenditure.

---

## Recommendations

The finance team recommends renegotiating the Microsoft Enterprise Agreement at renewal in February to target a 12% volume discount. Server refresh should be deferred to Q2 2027 based on current utilisation rates of 61% average across the fleet.
