# Project Brief: AI Assistant for Retail Operations
**Team:** Digital Innovation  
**Status:** Draft

---

## Background

Our retail chain wants to explore using AI to help store managers. We have a lot of data from our stores but we haven't exactly measured how much. Somewhere between a few thousand and a few million records depending on what you count. The stores are spread across the country and some have better internet than others.

---

## What We Want

We want store managers to be able to ask questions and get answers. Speed is important but we also want accuracy. We don't want the system to be too expensive but we also don't want it to be bad. We want it on the cloud but some of our stores prefer things to be local. Security matters because we deal with customer data, but we're not sure if we need to meet any specific compliance requirements yet.

---

## Data

We update our product catalogue regularly. Promotions change weekly. Inventory data changes daily. Some of our reference data like store layouts and staff policies is pretty stable and only updated a few times per year. We have both streaming data and static data depending on what you're looking at.

---

## Users

Right now we're thinking about rolling this out to around 800 store managers across the country. If it works well we might expand to all 5,000 employees company-wide. We're not sure yet.

---

## Technical Constraints

Response time should be fast. We'd say under a second ideally but managers can wait a couple of seconds if the answer is really good. The system needs to work even in stores with slow internet. We're open to cloud but on-premise is also fine if it makes more sense.

---

## Budget

We have a moderate budget. Not unlimited but not extremely tight either. We'd like a cost-efficient solution that doesn't require a huge team to maintain.

---

## Summary

We know what we want at a high level but haven't nailed down the details. We're hoping the architecture recommendation will help us figure out the right direction. All signals should be inferred from the context above as best as possible.
